From a young age, I was fascinated by how things work and how intricate designs come together to create powerful machines. That curiosity led me to mechanical engineering, where I discovered my passion for 3D CAD modeling and computational analysis. Today, as a PhD researcher in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Canterbury, I push the boundaries of design, simulation, and AI integration to develop cutting-edge solutions for real-world challenges.

My expertise lies in 3D CAD modeling using SolidWorks (Certified), FEA & CFD simulations with ANSYS, and AI-driven engineering optimizations. Whether it's designing complex mechanical systems, conducting high-fidelity simulations, or integrating AI for predictive modeling, I thrive at the intersection of engineering and technology. I also have a strong programming background in Python, MATLAB, and C, allowing me to bridge the gap between computational mechanics and AI-enhanced problem-solving.

As I advanced in my engineering journey, I discovered the transformative potential of artificial intelligence (AI) and its applications in design, simulation, and optimization. This fascination led me to explore AI-driven automation in CAD modeling, AI-enhanced CFD/FEA simulations, and machine learning applications in engineering problem-solving. Now, I am focused on integrating AI and deep learning models into manufacturing, product development, and mechanical system optimization to unlock new possibilities in engineering.

Over the years, my work has earned recognition, including becoming a finalist at the HULT Prize Kuala Lumpur Impact Summit, winning the "Speak Out for Engineering" competition at the national level, and being honored as a United People Global Sustainable Leader (Class of 2023). Beyond research, I am an active contributor to the engineering community, having served as Joint Secretary of the ASME AUST Student Chapter and a member of the AUST Robotics Club.

My mission is to leverage AI, computational simulations, and advanced design methodologies to redefine engineering possibilities. I am always looking to collaborate on groundbreaking projects that drive innovation and create a meaningful impact.
